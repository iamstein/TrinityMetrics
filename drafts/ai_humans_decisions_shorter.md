# AI Can Assist. Humans Must Own Decisions.

Generative AI is rapidly becoming capable of producing analyses, summaries, and even recommendations in scientific workflows.

This creates a risk that is easy to miss:  
**as analysis becomes easier to generate, responsibility can more easily become obscured.**

In regulated scientific work, this is not acceptable.

---

## Core Principle

At TrinityMetrics, we operate under a simple constraint:

> **AI may assist in analyzing data and assessing claims, but humans must explicitly own any finding that informs a decision.**

---

## What This Implies

### 1. Analysis and Decision Must Remain Distinct

AI can accelerate modeling, simulation, and exploratory analysis.  But concluding—turning results into a decision—remains a human responsibility.

---

### 2. Reasoning Must Be Visible

Conclusions should not be buried in outputs or reports.  It should be clear:

- What is being claimed  
- What supports the claim  
- What could be wrong  
- What the consequences are of being wrong  

Three frameworks that can help with the above are:

- [Context of Use Table](../references/ContextOfUse_Table_1page.pdf)
- [Uncertainty Checklist](../references/Uncertainty_Assessment_Pedigree_Table.pdf)
- [Validation Checklist](../references/Validation_Checklist.pdf)

---

### 3. More Output Is Not More Evidence

As AI-generated outputs become longer, smoother, and more persuasive,  
the burden on the reviewer increases—not decreases.  Persuasiveness is not validity.

---

## What TrinityMetrics Focuses On

Rather than producing abstract guidance, TrinityMetrics focuses on:

- Tools that reduce friction in real workflows  
- Patterns that make reasoning more transparent  
- Guardrails that preserve scientific and data integrity  

---

## Scope

The goal is not to automate judgment.

> **The goal is to accelerate analysis while ensuring that responsibility does not disappear with it.**

---

## Closing

TrinityMetrics explores how AI can be used in regulated scientific workflows—accelerating analysis without weakening accountability.
